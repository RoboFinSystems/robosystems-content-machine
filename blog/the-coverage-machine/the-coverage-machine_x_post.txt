

**1/**
There are thousands of public companies with real revenue and audited SEC filings — and zero analyst coverage.

The data isn't missing. It's public XBRL. What's missing is anyone turning it into something a human would watch.

So we built a machine that does. 🧵

**2/**
Give it a ticker. It pulls the company's actual SEC filings, runs a real financial analysis, and outputs a narrated video — plus a brief, a podcast, and the posts.

Research → Craft → Production.

Two stages automate. The middle one (design) stays human, on purpose.

**3/**
The discipline is the point.

Every number on screen traces to a line in a filing. We check the headline figures against the XBRL data before anything renders.

Valuation is a range under stated assumptions — never a price target, never advice.

**4/**
First campaign: US cannabis — the most under-covered corner of the market.

Latest: Trulieve. $1.2B revenue, 60% gross margin… and a reported loss, purely because an old tax rule taxed it at 228%.

That rate just collapsed to 87%. So — now what?
