
There are thousands of public companies with real revenue and audited SEC filings — and zero analyst coverage. No notes, no research, no one explaining what the numbers say.

The data isn't missing. Every filing is public, structured XBRL. What's missing is anyone turning it into something a human would actually watch.

So we built a machine that does.

Give it a ticker, and it pulls the company's actual SEC filings, runs a real financial analysis, and produces a narrated video breakdown — plus a written brief, a podcast, and the posts to go with it. Three stages:

→ Research — an AI analyst queries the structured filings (keyed to the company's permanent SEC ID), computes the trajectory, the cash flow, the tax math, the scenarios. Every number traces to a line in a filing.
→ Craft — the script becomes an on-brand deck. Human in the loop, on purpose.
→ Production — slice, voiceover, assemble. One command in, a finished 1080p video out.

The discipline is the point: every figure is checked against the source filing, and we frame valuation as a range under stated assumptions — never a price target, never advice. The viewer can verify everything.

First campaign is US cannabis. The latest: Trulieve — $1.2B in revenue, 60% gross margin, and a reported loss purely because an old tax rule taxed it at 228%. That rate just collapsed to 87%. So: now what?

The bottleneck on good financial analysis was never the analysis. It was the data being a mess. Fix the data, and coverage scales.
